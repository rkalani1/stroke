**Intracranial Hemorrhage**

1. Obtain:
   1. Neurologic symptoms + age, PMH, medications
   2. BP (presenting, current)
   3. Exam (focal deficits)
   4. PLT, Coags (INR, aPTT, DOAC screen [if available])
   5. Large volume hemorrhage or elderly 🡪 ensure goals of care concur with transfer and potential invasive procedures, but when in doubt err on the side of transfer and having conversations here (can be challenging to rely on non-neurology providers to accurately assess exam and convey neurologic prognosis)
   6. Any emergent medical concerns:
      1. Airway/breathing? Do they need to be intubated prior to transfer
      2. Herniating? Do you need to recommend mannitol (usually more available in ED) vs hypertonic saline?
      3. Suspected seizure? Give recommendations for treatment
      4. Any relevant infectious precautions (COVID?)
2. Recommend:
   1. BP target (labetalol? nicardipine gtt?)
   2. Anticoagulation reversal
   3. Imaging (repeat CT, MRI, DSA?)
   4. Whether to consult NSGY (does pt need an EVD, hemicraniectomy or if <80 yrs and lobar hemorrhage would they be a candidate for minimally invasive clot evacuation?)

OSH Transfers:

1. Remind to transfer via flight (unless UW-ML/NWH)
   1. If transfer from UW-ML or NWH please call the transfer center to let them know you are accepting and ensure transport is expediated (sometimes this means asking them to call 911).
2. Remind OSH to callback if new info OR clinical change
3. Check for updated ETA in the transfer center note timeline. They will update the ETA when they hear from Airlift.
4. Communicate with ER & neurology resident🡪 imaging upon arrival to HMC (ncHCT? CTA head?), BP goal, whether anticoagulation was reversed at the OSH, any of the emergent medical concerns above

**Ischemic Stroke / TIA**

1. Obtain:
   1. Neurologic symptoms + age, PMH, medications
   2. Last known normal
   3. BP (presenting, current)
   4. NIHSS + focal disabling deficits
   5. Blood glucose, PLT, coags (INR, aPTT)
   6. Non-con HCT
   7. CTA head/neck (+ CTP, if needed)
   8. Any emergent medical concerns:
      1. Airway/breathing? Do they need to be intubated prior to transfer
      2. Herniating? Do you need to recommend mannitol (usually more available in ED) vs hypertonic saline?
      3. Suspected seizure? Give recommendations for treatment
      4. Any relevant infectious precautions (COVID?)
2. Recommend:
   1. Reperfusion therapy 🡪 Thrombolytic? (if thrombolytic ensure contraindications/cautionary criteria reviewed to best ability) EVT?
   2. Antithrombotic tx (single or DAPT and recommend dose, loading doses if appropriate)
   3. BP target (treat if BP>220/120, >180/105 if thrombolytic administered)
   4. MRI, TTE, EKG, telemetry, lipids/HgA1c, NPO pending swallow
   5. Whether to consult NSGY

OSH Transfers:

1. Transfer TNK & EVT cases via flight (unless UW-ML/NWH)
   1. If transfer from UW-ML please call the transfer center to let them know you are accepting and ensure transport is expediated (sometimes this means asking them to call 911).
2. Remind OSH to callback if new info OR clinical change
3. Check for updated ETA in the transfer center note timeline. They will update the ETA when they hear from Airlift.
4. Ensure images do get pushed into Visage. If not, call TC to troubleshoot.
5. Communicate with neurology resident via phone and via ER/others via Pulsara 🡪 Include in Pulsara brief clinical hx age, relevant risk factors, presenting sx, NIHSS, any emergent medical concerns, OSH imaging findings, and plan for arrival (usually exam and imaging)
   1. If a LVO likely to go from thrombectomy give the IR MD on call a heads up. During the day an acknowledged text or a phone call when we are aware VS if after 10PM CALL closer to pt’s anticipated arrival.

Telestroke Note:

1. Document times 🡪 LKN, BP review, NIHSS completed, HCT/CTA review, TNK recommendation, TNK administered (if you are on the call when given)
