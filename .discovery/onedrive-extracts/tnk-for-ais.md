**Intravenous Tenecteplase (TNK) for Acute Ischemic Stroke**

**Protocol for Harborview Medical Center & University of Washington Medical Center**

**1. Dosing & Administration**

**Tenecteplase Dose:** 0.25 mg/kg intravenous bolus

**Maximum Dose:** 25 mg

**2. Inclusion Criteria**

* Age ≥ 18 years (may be considered off-label in children age 2 years or older)
* Clinical diagnosis of ischemic stroke causing a measurable, disabling neurologic deficit
* CT brain excludes intracranial hemorrhage
* Onset of symptoms within 4.5 hours of the time to initiation of treatment with intravenous TNK
  + FDA approved for administration within 3 hours of symptom onset; review cautionary criteria for use in the 3 – 4.5-hour time window

**Wake-Up Stroke, Unknown Onset, or 4.5–24 Hour Since Last Known Well\***

If no LVO (and/or EVT not planned) – may be considered for IV thrombolytic treatment based on advanced imaging selection criteria:

* **MRI-Based Selection:**
  + Code Stroke MRI demonstrating restricted diffusion WITHOUT any evident corresponding T2/FLAIR signal hyperintensity (DWI-FLAIR mismatch)
* **CT Perfusion-Based Selection:**

| **Parameter** | **Criteria** |
| --- | --- |
| Core volume (CBV < 30%) | < 70 mL |
| Penumbra – Core (absolute mismatch) | ≥ 10 mL |
| Penumbra/Core (mismatch ratio) | > 1.2 |

\*4.5-24h window treatment is not yet included in AHA guidelines. Optimal selection criteria for late time window advanced imaging selection is still being defined. Trials showing benefit thus far, have been conducted in China and included patients with median NIHSS 7-11 and infarct core <1-16 mL. Symptomatic ICH was statistically higher in the HOPE trial 3.8% in tPA group vs 0.5% standard medical and numerically higher but not statistically significant in TRACE III.

**3. Exclusion Criteria**

* **3 – 4.5-hour time window: additional cautionary criteria**
  + Age > 80 years
  + History of both diabetes mellitus AND prior stroke
  + Baseline NIHSS > 25
  + Evidence of ischemic injury involving > 1/3 of the MCA territory
  + Oral anticoagulant use
* **Absolute Contraindications:**
  + Imaging & Historical Exclusions
    - CT scan with evidence of hemorrhage
    - CT demonstrates extensive regions of clear hypoattenuation
    - Clinical presentation suggestive of subarachnoid hemorrhage, even if initial CT scan is normal
    - Ischemic stroke within the previous 90 days
    - History of severe head trauma within the previous 90 days
    - Intracranial or intraspinal surgery within the past 60 days
    - Unruptured and unsecured large intracranial aneurysm (> 10 mm)
    - Intracranial, intra-axial tumor (note: extra-axial tumors are likely acceptable)
    - Aortic arch dissection
  + Clinical Exclusions
    - Hypertension with systolic blood pressure > 185 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure > 110 mmHg on repeated measures prior to starting TNK, unresponsive to antihypertensive therapy
    - Active internal bleeding
    - History of gastrointestinal malignancy or recent gastrointestinal or urinary tract hemorrhage within 21 days
    - Arterial puncture at a non-compressible site within the last 7 days
    - Blood glucose < 50 mg/dL
    - Symptoms consistent with or known infective endocarditis
    - Thrombolytics are not recommend in patients receiving anti-amyloid therapies (lecanemab, donanemab), but please confirm decision with stroke phone and prepare for potential urgent MRI (acute ARIA-related symptoms may mimic ischemic stroke)
  + Coagulation & Anticoagulant-Related Exclusions
    - Platelet count < 100,000/mm³ \*
    - Use of warfarin with prolonged PT > 15 seconds, INR > 1.7, or aPTT > 40 seconds
    - Confirmed or suspected use of a direct thrombin inhibitor (e.g., dabigatran) within 48 hours, assuming normal renal function \*\*
    - Confirmed or suspected use of a direct factor Xa inhibitor (e.g., rivaroxaban, apixaban, edoxaban) within 48 hours, assuming normal renal function \*\*
    - Abnormal values in the Emergency Stroke Panel (includes TT, PT, aPTT, and anti-Xa assay)
    - Use of treatment-dose heparin or LMWH in the previous 24 hours

\* In patients without a known history of thrombocytopenia, IV TNK may be initiated before the platelet count returns; however, TNK should be discontinued if the platelet count returns < 100,000/mm³.

\*\* For direct thrombin inhibitors: If the thrombin time (TT) assay is normal (not elevated/prolonged), little or no dabigatran effect is present. The TT assay typically takes less than 10 minutes and is included in the Emergency Stroke Panel. For direct factor Xa inhibitors: The anti-Xa assay provides indication of Xa inhibitor activity and is included in the Emergency Stroke Panel.

**4. Relative Exclusion (Cautionary) Criteria**

While absolute contraindications, these factors may increase the risk of symptomatic ICH and warrant careful individual risk-benefit assessment:

* Prior known non-traumatic intracranial hemorrhage
* Known vascular malformation, unless neurological deficits are severe such that ischemic risks outweigh those of ICH
* Pregnancy: TNK has been administered and is reasonable when anticipated benefits outweigh the potential risks of uterine bleeding. Consultation with Obstetrics/Gynecology should be obtained as soon as possible.
* Major extra-cranial surgery or significant trauma within 14 days
* Acute or recent myocardial infarction within 3 months, depending on the type of MI (lower-level evidence supports use of TNK in these settings)
* Acute pericarditis
* Abnormal aPTT, TT, or anti-Xa activity with unknown use of direct thrombin inhibitor or factor Xa inhibitor (may represent false positive due to lupus anticoagulant; consider IV TNK if able to reliably confirm the patient is not taking one of these agents)
* Cerebral microbleeds: > 10 known microbleeds on prior MRI may increase risk for ICH

**5. Documentation Reminders**

* Ensure consent is obtained when able and documented in clinical note, but do not delay lytic administration if patient is unable to consent and family is not available, as thrombolytics are standard of care.
* Ensure the lytic safety pause is completed prior to thrombolytic administration and documented in clinical note (.UWMSTROKESAFETYPAUSE.
