**Communication with Neurosurgery**

* The stroke phone attending must approve all consultations with neurosurgery for patients with intraparenchymal hemorrhage or ischemic stroke. The stroke phone attending/fellow may contact the admitting attending for additional input, if needed. The admitting resident or stroke phone should inform the attending neurology attending of surgical plans to ensure agreement of the treatment plan.
  + If the admitting neurology attending prefers to be involved in decision making, they should contact the stroke phone attending/fellow(s) to inform them of this preference when on clinical service.
  + The neurology resident should evaluate the patient and discuss all cases with the stroke phone (and/or admitting) attending prior to any neurosurgery consultation.
* No "curbside" consults, "heads-up," or "FYI" calls to neurosurgery.
* If the stroke/admitting attending recommends against surgical intervention (based on the clinical evaluation, care goals, etc.), neurosurgery should not be consulted.
* If there is disagreement about operative intervention, the stroke/admitting neurology attending, and trauma neurosurgery attending will discuss the case by phone to make a final decision.
  + The trauma neurosurgery attending can be paged via the HMC operator or EMR (On-Call Finder) or can be contacted through the neurosurgery resident.
* Intraparenchymal Hemorrhage:
  + If an external ventricular drain and/or decompressive hemicraniectomy is requested, the neurology resident will consult the neurosurgery resident on call. If there is uncertainty as to whether a procedure is of benefit, indicate that the opinion of the neurosurgery attending is requested.
  + Please see separate document regarding consultation for minimally invasive clot evacuation
* Ischemic Stroke:
  + If decompressive hemicraniectomy for malignant hemispheric or cerebellar/brainstem infarction is requested, the neurology resident will consult neurosurgery resident on call. If there is uncertainty as to whether a procedure will be beneficial, specify that the opinion of the neurosurgery attending is requested.
* Neurology should not recommend surgical procedures to patients and/or their families without a finalized and mutually agreed upon plan with neurosurgery.
