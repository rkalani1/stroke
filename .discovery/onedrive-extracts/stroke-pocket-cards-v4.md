## Card 1 — IVT Eligibility Decision Algorithm

**Suspected Stroke**
↓
**Focused history + NIHSS + NCCT Head + Labs**
- Stroke panel: PT/INR, PTT, anti-Xa, CBC, glucose, BMP
- Glucose >50 or >400? Correct first; proceed with IVT if deficits persist
↓
**ICH on CT?**
- **YES** → IVT not recommended
- **NO** → continue

**Disabling deficit?** (Would deficits impair ADLs or return to work? bathe / bathroom / ambulating / toileting / hygiene / eating)
- **NO** → IVT NOT recommended (COR 3: NB, LOE B-NR; consider per SAFT)
- **YES** → continue

**Review Contraindications** (relative — individualize risk/benefit)

**Time from LKN?**
- **≤ 4.5 h** → **TNK RECOMMENDED** (COR 1, LOE A)
- **> 4.5 h** → **Advanced Imaging Selection** (CTP or MRI DWI-FLAIR mismatch required; prefer MRI if small vessel, posterior circ, contrast allergy)
  - **4.5-9h or wake-up (MRI)** → **CONSIDER TNK** (COR 2a, LOE B-R)
  - **9-24h (CTP)** → **CONSIDER TNK** • Consent REQUIRED
    - 4.5-24h Criteria: baseline mRS ≤ 1; CTP: core < 50 mL, ratio ≥ 1.2, mismatch volume ≥ 15 mL; NOT in CKD/avoid contrast
    - IVT may be considered in select patients with CTP/MRI not done if a perfusion deficit is suspected (e.g., disabling NIHSS, AIS in clinic)

**TNK Dosing:** 0.25 mg/kg IV push (max 25 mg)
**Safety pause:** confirm consent, BP < 185/110, review contraindications.
("UNRESTRAINED SAFETY PAUSE" obtain consent for IVT)

**Central Retinal Artery Occlusion (CRAO):** Consider IVT in select cases with informed consent and shared decision-making. Trial evidence does not support efficacy.

## Card 2 — IVT Eligibility (contraindications detail)

### ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS
| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| CT with hemorrhage | Acute intracranial hemorrhage on imaging |
| CT with extensive hypodensity | Clear hypodensity responsible for symptoms |
| Acute spinal cord injury < 3 months | Intracranial/spinal surgery within 14 days — potentially harmful |
| Intra-axial neoplasm | Likely contraindicated |
| Infective endocarditis | Should not be administered |
| Severe coagulopathy | Plt <100K, INR >1.7, aPTT >40s, PT >15s |
| Aortic arch dissection | Potentially harmful — should not be administered |
| Moderate-severe TBI < 14 days | GCS <13 OR hemorrhage/contusion/skull fracture |
| Amyloid immunotherapy / ARIA | ICH risk unknown — IV thrombolysis should be avoided |

### RELATIVE CONTRAINDICATIONS / INDIVIDUALIZE RISK/BENEFIT
| Item | Treatment-on-individual-basis note |
|---|---|
| Pre-existing disability/frailty | — |
| DOAC exposure < 48 h | Potential efficacy supported by observational studies |
| Prior ischemic stroke < 3 months | Weigh timing/size against IVT benefit |
| Prior ICH | Amyloid angiopathy = higher risk; modifiable causes (HTN) may have greater benefit |
| Major non-CNS trauma (14d-3mo) | Surgical consultation; consider involved areas |
| Major non-CNS surgery < 10 days | Consider surgical area and risk |
| GI/GU bleeding < 21 days | GI/GU consultation |
| Arterial/dural puncture < 7 days | May be considered in individual cases |
| Intracranial vascular malformations | Safety unknown; unruptured and untreated |
| Intracranial arterial dissection | Safety unknown |
| Recent STEMI < 3 months | Cardiology consult; hemopericardium risk |
| Acute pericarditis | May be reasonable; emergent cardiology consult |
| Left atrial or ventricular thrombus | May be reasonable if major AIS; cardiology consult |
| Pregnancy / post-partum | Obstetrics consultation; benefits vs uterine bleeding risk |
| Systemic active malignancy | Oncology consultation; consider type, stage, complications |
| Neurosurgery 14 days-3 months | Individual basis; neurosurgical consultation recommended |
| Moderate-severe TBI 14 days-3 months | Consider type/severity; neurosurgical consult |

### BENEFIT GENERALLY > RISK · CONSIDER IVT
| Item | Note |
|---|---|
| Extracranial cervical dissection | Reasonably safe within 4.5h; probably recommended |
| Extra-axial intracranial neoplasm | Benefit likely outweighs risk |
| Unruptured intracranial aneurysm | Benefit likely outweighs risk; treatment should be considered |
| Angiographic procedural stroke | Benefit likely outweighs risk |
| History of GI/GU bleeding (remote) | Candidates if stable; GI/GU consultation |
| History of MI (remote) | Probably greater benefit than risk |
| Recreational drug use | Greater benefit than risk in most patients |
| Stroke mimic uncertainty | Risk of harm with IVT is low; benefit likely outweighs risk |
| Moya-Moya disease | No increased ICH risk; benefit likely outweighs risk |

## Card 3 — DOAC Exposed Patients: IVT

**AIS patient with DOAC exposure < 48h**
↓
Criteria:
- Disabling stroke deficits
- NOT an endovascular candidate
- All other IVT exclusion criteria reviewed
↓
**HMC / UWML / UWNW** — Additional requirement: **Anti-Xa UNDETECTABLE**
(documentation: attending attestation recommended)

**Telestroke Sites** — Additional requirements:
- Normal renal function
- Last known dose ≥ 24 hours
- **Consent REQUIRED**
↓
**IVT may be considered**
Based on observational data only — no RCTs.

### Patient Discussion: IV Thrombolysis in the Extended Window (4.5-24 Hours)

> Tenecteplase, a clot-dissolving medication, is a treatment for your suspected stroke that can be given beyond the usual 4.5-hour window when brain imaging shows there is still tissue that can be saved. In clinical trials, people who received this treatment in the extended time window had about a 9-11% better chance of recovering without disability compared with people who did not receive the treatment. However, all medicines have some risk, and with clot-dissolving drugs, there is a risk of serious bleeding. Bleeding into the brain that leads to new symptoms can occur in up to about 3% of those treated. This use of clot-dissolving medications beyond 4.5 hours from stroke onset is supported by limited evidence from clinical trials and many uncertainties remain. Your alternatives include standard stroke care without thrombolysis. Based on your imaging findings and clinical presentation, we suspect the benefit of treatment outweighs the risk and recommend proceeding with Tenecteplase treatment.

| | | | NIHSS 6-9 (effectiveness not well established) | | | |

**Footnote definitions:**
- LVO = ICA or proximal M1 MCA
- ‡ Dominant proximal M2 MCA occlusion: M2 branch ≥ 1 cm from MCA bifurcation, supplying ≥ 50% MCA territory
- DISTAL = Mid M2/distal occlusions or trial criteria: ICA + tissue at risk < 100 mL; codominant M2: 60-100 mL, no mismatch; M2 < 80 mL
- Recommendations assume baseline mRS 0-1 unless otherwise specified
- mRS (modified Rankin Scale, baseline pre-stroke): 0 — no symptoms; 1 — no significant disability; 2 — slight disability; 3 — moderate, walks unaided; 4 — moderately severe, needs assistance; 5 — severe, bedridden; 6 — dead
- Generalizability limited for: age > 90, significant headwind reverse tortuosity, comorbidities affecting assessment, seizures at stroke onset, high suspicion for underlying ICAD

## Card 5 — Class of Recommendation · Level of Evidence

| Class of Recommendation | Benefit/Risk | Wording |
|---|---|---|
| **CLASS 1** (Strong) | Benefit >>> Risk | Is recommended |
| **CLASS 2a** (Moderate) | Benefit >> Risk | Is reasonable |
| **CLASS 2b** (Weak) | Benefit ≥ Risk | May be considered |
| **CLASS 3: No Benefit** (Moderate) | Benefit = Risk | Not recommended |
| **CLASS 3: Harm** (Strong) | Risk > Benefit | Avoid (harmful) |

| Level of Evidence | Description |
|---|---|
| **LEVEL A** | High-quality RCTs / meta-analyses |
| **LEVEL B-R** | Moderate-quality RCTs |
| **LEVEL B-NR** | Well-designed observational/registry |
| **LEVEL C-LD** | Limited data / mechanistic studies |
| **LEVEL C-EO** | Expert opinion / consensus |

## Card 5 (continued) — Blood Pressure Management

| Scenario | Target | COR | LOE | HMC Protocol Details |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Before IVT | BP < 185/110 | COR 1 | B-NR | Labetalol 10 mg IV, repeat q15 min; increase to 20 mg, then 40 mg, then 60 mg (max bolus). Max 300 mg in 2h. |
| After IVT (24h) | BP < 180/105 | COR 1 | B-R | Monitor q15 min × 2h → q30 min × 6h → q1h until 24h. Treat if SBP > 180 or DBP > 105. |
| After EVT (24h) | BP < 180/105 | COR 2a | B-NR | For successful recanalization (mTICI ≥ 2b). Target range SBP 140-180. |
| **SBP < 140 after IVT or EVT** | — | — | — | **After IVT: Not recommended** — no functional improvement vs < 180 target (COR 3: NB, LOE B-R). **After EVT: HARMFUL for 72h** — ↓ functional independence, ↑ mortality (COR 3: Harm, LOE A) |
| Ischemic (no reperfusion) | BP < 220/120 | — | — | No benefit to initiating treatment if BP < 220/120 without comorbid indication (COR 3: NB, LOE A) |
