Intracranial Hemorrhage

1. Obtain:
   1. Neurologic symptoms + age, PMH, medications
   2. BP (presenting, current)
   3. Exam (focal deficits)
   4. PLT, Coags (INR, aPTT)
   5. Large volume hemorrhage or elderly 🡪 clarify care goals (EVD / decompressive crani?)
2. Recommend:
   1. BP target (nicardipine gtt?)
   2. AC reversal or PLT transfusion (if applicable)
   3. Imaging (repeat CT, MRI, DSA?)
   4. Whether to consult NSGY

OSH Transfers:

1. Remind to transfer via flight (unless UW-ML/NWH)
2. Remind OSH to callback if new info OR clinical change
3. Ask transfer center to page/call with ETA
4. Communicate with ER & neurology resident🡪 imaging upon arrival to HMC (ncHCT? CTA head?)

Ischemic Stroke / TIA:

1. Obtain:
   1. Neurologic symptoms + age, PMH, medications
   2. Last known normal
   3. BP (presenting, current)
   4. NIHSS + focal disabling deficits
   5. BG, PLT, coags (INR, aPTT)
   6. Non-con HCT
   7. CTA head/neck (+ CTP, if needed)
   8. Large volume infarction 🡪 clarify care goals (decompressive crani?)
2. Recommend:
   1. Reperfusion therapy 🡪 TNK? EVT?
   2. Antithrombotic tx (single or DAPT)
   3. BP target (treat if BP>220/120, >180/105 if TNK administered)
   4. MRI, TTE, EKG, telemetry, lipids/HgA1c, NPO pending swallow
   5. Whether to consult NSGY

OSH Transfers:

1. Transfer TNK & EVT cases via flight (unless UW-ML/NWH)
2. Remind OSH to callback if new info OR clinical change
3. Ask transfer center to page/call with ETA
4. Communicate with ER & neurology resident via Pulsara 🡪 imaging upon arrival to HMC (ncHCT? CTA head?)

TNK:

1. Confirm disabling deficit & infusion started ≤4.5 hours from LKN
2. Review exclusions: ICH, SBP>185 or DBP>110 persistently, warfarin / heparin use + INR>1.7 / PT>15 / aPTT >40, DOAC use + laboratory evidence of AC effect, treatment dose LMWH ≤24 hours, PLT<100, active hemorrhage, prior ICH or intra-axial neoplasm, GI malignancy, ischemic stroke / severe head trauma ≤3 months, cranial / spinal surgery ≤3 months, GI hemorrhage ≤21 days, suspected infective endocarditis / aortic dissection
3. Risks/benefit discussion w/ patient/family 🡪 "We recommend a medication call Tenecteplase (TNK), which is a “clot-buster” drug. TNK reduces the risk of being disabled; people who get TNK have an improved chance of recovering without disability than people who don't get the medication. All medications have risks – the main risk is bleeding, up to 4-5% of people develop bleeding in the brain that leads to new symptoms. Very rarely, people have an allergic reaction. The potential benefits are thought to be higher than the potential risks and we recommend TNK is administered. Time is very important here - the sooner the treatment is started, the higher the likelihood of benefit."
4. Post-TNK recommendations: maintain BP<180/105 before, during, and x 24 hours post-infusion. If new HA or neurological deterioration - STAT HCT. No antithrombotic tx or ppx AC x 24 hours.

\*LKN unclear (not awake/known to have had sx for 4.5 hours) + NO LVO: MRI w/ DWI and T2/FLAIR; discuss ↓ certainty in benefit and ↑ hemorrhage risk w/ thrombolysis
