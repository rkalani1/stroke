**Mission Statement**: [One concise sentence that captures the (department’s/division’s/unit’s) most authentic and compelling reason for being. Brief and clear, bold yet humble, motivating, intriguing]

**Provide the best care possible to patients with the full spectrum of stroke-related diseases throughout the course of their illness**

**Provide:**

* Comprehensive, high-quality, evidence-based stroke care to meet local & regional needs of patients and providers
* Stroke education and guidance for secondary prevention to patients and providers
* Training the next generation of stroke clinicians
* Excellence in stroke research

**Vision Statement**: [Short and aspirational – describes the ideal condition you seek to create through your work. Memorable, present tense, easy to understand, uniquely differentiate, capture your ideal north star and feeling you want to convey]

**Serve as a beacon of stroke care, education, and research throughout the region.**

**Goals & Strategies:**

**Clinical care**

* Provide high-quality clinical care to individuals with stroke and those at high risk for vascular brain injury.
  + Deliver acute and preventive stroke care, informed by the best available evidence, that is concordant with individual patient goals and values.
  + Expand telemedicine partnerships with hospitals across the WWAMI region to provide access to timely expert stroke care, especially to underserved populations across the region.

**Research**

* Conduct innovative scientific research to improve stroke prevention, therapy, and recovery.
  + Expand basic, translational, clinical, and epidemiologic research programs focused on vascular brain injury.
  + Participate in national and international clinical trials focused on stroke prevention, acute treatment, and recovery.
  + Establish new interdisciplinary collaborations to expand the scope and impact of stroke research across UW.

**Education**

* Train the next generation of healthcare professionals and scientists focused on reducing the burden of vascular brain injury.
  + Provide comprehensive education to vascular neurology fellows and neurology residents through experiential learning and graded independence in stroke patient care.
  + Promote a culture of continual learning among all healthcare providers through structured didactics and the development of just-in-time learning programs.
  + Offer regular Continuing Medical Education opportunities for healthcare providers at UW and across the region to disseminate the latest advancements in stroke medicine.
  + Enhance educational outreach programs to raise stroke awareness and prevention in the local community and underserved populations across the region.

**Culture**

* Foster a culture of creativity and opportunity to attract, retain, and develop the stroke center faculty, trainees, and staff.
  + Promote a collaborative, engaging, and inclusive work culture.
  + Enhance professional development and identify leadership roles for interested team members.
  + Provide opportunities and mentorship for junior and mid-career faculty and staff to participate in stroke center activities.
  + Ensure individual and collective efforts are recognized.
  + Encourage the development of new clinical and academic initiatives that promote our mission and vision.
