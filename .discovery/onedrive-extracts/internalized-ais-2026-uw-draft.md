**DOACs** are now a relative contraindication to thrombolytics but this is based only on observational trials.

Approach to considering lytics in patients on DOACs. All usual exclusion criteria and cautionary criteria still apply.

**HMC/UWML/UWNW**:

-Disabling deficits and NOT an endovascular candidate

-anti-Xa is undetectable

**Telestroke sites**:

-Disabling deficits and NOT an endovascular candidate

-normal renal function

-Last known dose $\geq $ 24h

**Extended window thrombolytics: 4.5-9h or wake-up/unknown last know well**. Consider CTP or MRI for selection of patients who qualify for thrombolytics.

Preference for MRI when suspicion is for small vessel infarct, posterior circulation, or if pt has a severe contrast allergy.

**Telestroke sites (Confluence)**, Consider if:

-Disabling stroke deficits

-Not a candidate for endovascular intervention

-baseline mRS $\leq $ 1

-CTP with core <50mL, mismatch volume $\geq 1.2$ and mismatch volume $\geq $ 10mL

-Consent must be documented in telestroke note

AND

-Disabling stroke deficits

-LVO but transport is not there and transfer anticipated to be >1h

-baseline mRS $\leq $ 1

-CTP with core <50mL, mismatch volume $\geq 1.2$ and mismatch volume $\geq $ 10mL

-Consent must be documented in telestroke note

Guidance for consent: “Getting this clot buster after a stroke reduces your risk of disability. For people who get this medicine within 4.5 hours after stroke there is strong, clear evidence of better recovery and getting back to the activities you love. You are outside that traditional window, but because your imaging suggests you have brain tissue at risk, that we might still be able to save, there is emerging evidence that clot busters can be given past that 4.5-hour window. This is not as established as within the early time window, and this is not without risk. The main risk is of serious bleeding, including a risk of bleeding within the brain. We think you would be a candidate for this treatment if you would find living with your current [insert stroke symptoms here] disabling or if it would significantly limit your ability to enjoy your life.”
