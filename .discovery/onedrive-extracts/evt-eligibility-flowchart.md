## EVT Eligibility Criteria

### Vessel Occlusion Type?

**Basilar Artery Occlusion**
- Last known well ≤ 24 hours
- NIHSS score ≥ 10
- pc-ASPECTS ≥ 6

**Anterior Circulation Large Vessel Occlusion**
- Intracranial ICA or M1 segment MCA occlusion
- Trial evidence principally supports efficacy among:
  - Adults ≤ 80 years of age, baseline mRS 0-1
  - NIHSS ≥ 6 with disabling symptoms

**Medium Vessel Occlusion**
- Consider in select cases of proximal or dominant M2 segment MCA occlusion ≤ 1 cm of bifurcation in horizontal segment when there is evidence of salvageable tissue + last known well ≤ 24 hours
- Not recommended for M2-M4 MCA, ACA, and PCA occlusions

### Time from symptom onset?

**Early Window (0-6h)**
- ASPECTS 3-10: generally eligible for EVT
- ASPECTS 0-2: consider in very select cases; consider CTP to evaluate if estimated core volume is ≤ 70-100 cc

**Late Window (6-24h)**
- ASPECTS 6-10: generally eligible for EVT
- ASPECTS 3-5: generally eligible for EVT; consider CTP to evaluate if estimated core volume is ≤ 100 cc + mismatch is present
- ASPECTS 0-2: EVT benefit is unclear

### Footnotes / cautions
- These criteria are guidelines; clinical judgment is essential.
- Consider patient-specific factors in all decisions (e.g., age > 90 with low ASPECTS score would not generally be a good candidate).
- Neuro-IR attending should be in agreement with pursuing EVT.
