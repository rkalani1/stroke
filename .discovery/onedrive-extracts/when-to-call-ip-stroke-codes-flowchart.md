# Re-envisioned Stoke Code Proposed Workflow

<!-- Slide number: 9 -->
When to consider a stroke code?

![A close-up of a sign Description automatically generated](Picture15.jpg)

Neurologic symptoms with a last known well ≤24 hours that are:
FOCAL (e.g. unilateral weakness, language/speech disturbance, significant vision change, abrupt onset coma)
DISABLING (e.g. a deficit that would prevent performance of basic activities of daily living or returning to work)
NEW (seen without symptoms within ≤24 hours)

NO or UNSURE
Call Rapid Response Team AND page primary team for STAT assessment
Rapid Response Team will initiate stroke code if indicated

Think, Pause, and Discuss

Symptoms/signs known to be present >24h
Non-disabling symptoms (isolated dysarthria, facial droop, sensory symptoms, altered mental status without focal deficit)
Known alternative etiology (delirium, sepsis, recent sedation)
Seizure with known epilepsy
If STAT brain imaging  needed (but acute stroke not suspected) – Order STAT CT head AND see imaging escalation process

YES

Call Rapid Response Team (they will activate stroke code)
Assess ABCs (airway, breathing, circulation) + GLUCOSE
Call primary Team STAT to bedside
Primary team  initiate EPIC GEN IP code stroke order set
Draw POC glucose and “emergency stroke panel”
Have last known well time, new identified symptoms, and relevant medical history ready
Any anticoagulation, recent surgery, recent major hemorrhage?
