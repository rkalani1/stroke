**Direct to Angio for LVO pts without lytics and within 3h of last neuroimaging**

Why the suggested change: Goal door to puncture time for transfer LVO cases $\leq $ 45 minutes and historically we do not consistently meet this metric. Additional imaging obtained in the ED is time consuming and given the benefits of thrombectomy even in the setting of large core, we should be willing to forgo repeat imaging in some patients.

**Proposed change:**

For medically and neurologically STABLE patients coming from UWML, UW-NW, and other transfer sites where the last neuroimaging was within 3h we should strive to go directly to angio.

-Assessment at triage as pt arrives ED, Neuro, IR. Neuro exam would happen here and if all deem pt is stable for angio, pt would be directly transported to IR suite.

-This will NOT apply to:

* Patients with clinical exam improvement such that symptoms no longer meet our usual intervention standard of NIHSS $\geq $6 with disabling symptoms
* Patients with neurologic worsening en route defined by NIHSS $\geq $4 or concerns raised by bedside providers
* Patients receiving lytics at the OSH
* Any patient where ED/Neuro/IR is concerned there is an unstable medical issue
