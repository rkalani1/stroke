Initial non-traumatic IPH evaluation
Reviewed
2026-07-03
Institution-neutral educational translation. Use this as a workflow checklist only; verify against the current approved local protocol before clinical action.
Non-traumatic IPH >=15 mL by ABC/2 prompts early Neurosurgery and stroke-service evaluation.
ED clinicians or the stroke service may call Neurosurgery directly; prior approval is not required, but the plan must be closed-looped with the designated on-call stroke attending and other involved service.
Transfers and first contact
•
Start standard stabilization: agent-specific reversal, controlled BP lowering, head-of-bed elevation, and osmotic therapy when indicated.
•
Confirm last-known-well time, estimated arrival time, antithrombotic exposure, and legal next-of-kin/contact information.
•
Notify the receiving ED, stroke service, and ICU admitting service for ICU-level admissions.
ED diagnosis or arrival
•
Measure hematoma volume using ABC/2 and treat >=15 mL as the early Neurosurgery + stroke-service evaluation threshold.
•
ED clinicians or the stroke service may consult Neurosurgery directly; prior approval is not required.
•
Whichever service calls Neurosurgery closes the loop with the designated on-call stroke attending and other involved service so the plan is shared and documented.
•
Separate attending-of-record notification is not default unless that expectation is explicitly requested, especially overnight.
•
Consult earlier at any size for IVH, hydrocephalus, cerebellar hemorrhage, mass effect, neurologic decline, concerning pupillometry trend/asymmetry, multicompartmental hemorrhage, vascular lesion concern, ED attending discretion, or clinician concern.
CTA/MRA branch
•
If CTA/MRA shows an underlying vascular lesion or anomaly, the Neurosurgery/neurointerventional pathway leads admission or operative/interventional planning with neurocritical care or ICU support.
•
If there is no vascular lesion and no surgical pathway, the stroke service leads admission with ICU support as needed.
Monitoring adjuncts
•
Use close serial neurologic exams and repeat imaging when the exam changes.
•
Use pupillometry where available as an adjunct for patients at risk of hydrocephalus, herniation, or neurologic deterioration.

Trial and Registry Screens
MINUTE screen
Age 18-80; Spontaneous non-traumatic supratentorial non-thalamic basal-ganglia IPH; Volume >=15 mL by ABC/2, or close enough to prompt screening; NIHSS >=6; CTA/MRA without vascular lesion; Arrival/evaluation <=15 hours since last known well; No clear standard-of-care surgical indication

Do not let registry screening delay urgent surgical, trial, or stabilization decisions.

ICH First-Hour Critical Bundle
AHA/ASA 2022 + INTERACT3 + ENRICH (rapid workflow card)
1. Airway and Neuro ICU Escalation
Escalate airway and ICU-level monitoring early when exam worsens or airway protection is uncertain.
2. Blood Pressure Strategy
IIa
Use smooth, sustained BP control and timely treatment; target SBP 140 with range 130-150 when appropriate, and avoid acute SBP <130.
Evidence
AHA/ASA 2022: smooth, sustained BP control and timely treatment are Class IIa; for mild-moderate ICH with SBP 150-220, target 140 (range 130-150) is Class IIb; acute SBP <130 is Class III harm. INTERACT3 tested a care bundle, not BP alone.
3. Immediate Anticoag Reversal
I
If anticoagulant exposure is possible, initiate agent-specific reversal without delay using the pathways below.
Evidence
AHA/ASA 2022: Class I for VKA reversal with IV vitamin K + 4-factor PCC. Class IIa for idarucizumab (dabigatran). Andexanet alfa (Xa inhibitors) considered but institutional protocols may prefer PCC.
4. Core Support Bundle
Treat fever and hyperglycemia, monitor sodium/osmolality when indicated, and prevent secondary injury.
5. Early Surgery Screen
IIa
Promptly screen for neurosurgical escalation in cerebellar decline, hydrocephalus, mass effect, or selected lobar large ICH.
Evidence
ENRICH (2024): Early MIS for selected lobar ICH in the 30-80 mL trial range improved outcomes vs medical management (mRS shift OR 0.74). STICH II: Open craniotomy for lobar ICH did not show clear benefit. Cerebellar hemorrhage with posterior-fossa mass effect, usually with obstructive hydrocephalus and/or brainstem compression: urgent suboccipital decompression screen.
Conservative default: stabilize, control BP, reverse anticoagulation, and escalate ICU/surgical pathways before lower-priority tasks.

Minimally Invasive Evacuation (MIE)
Surgical Selection
June 2026 operational MIE screen: lobar IPH 30-80 mL, NIHSS >5, GCS 5-14, age 18-80, and no underlying lesion.
General guideline evidence supports selected minimally invasive evacuation for supratentorial ICH, especially for mortality; older/general guideline framing kept broad functional-outcome benefit uncertain.
ENRICH supports selected lobar 30-80 mL patients; use the June 2026 screen below and confirm neurosurgery/local-protocol activation.
MIS may be reasonable over conventional craniotomy in eligible patients; do not generalize outside the June 2026 screen.

ENRICH MIE Exclusion
• Coagulopathy
• IVH >50% either lateral ventricle
• Thalamic or infratentorial ICH
• Underlying structural lesion (tumor, AVM)

ICH Volume Calculation:
ABC/2 Method:
A = largest ICH diameter (cm)
B = largest diameter 90 degrees to A on same slice (cm)
C = (# CT slices with ICH) x (slice thickness)
Volume = A x B x C / 2

Hematoma Expansion Prevention
Blood Pressure Target
Target:
SBP 140 mmHg, maintain 130-150 when appropriate, with timely smooth control. Avoid SBP <130 (ATACH-2).
Smooth control:
avoid peaks and variability.
SBP >220:
Safety of intensive lowering is uncertain (Class IIb, LOE C-EO, AHA 2022). Reasonable to target modest reduction (SBP 140-160) using continuous IV infusion with close monitoring. Avoid rapid drops >60 mmHg in the first hour. Consider starting nicardipine at a lower rate (2.5-5 mg/hr) and titrating slowly.
Agent:
IV nicardipine or clevidipine for titration.
Expansion Risk Factors
• Spot sign on CTA
• Anticoagulant use
• Time from onset <3 hours
• Large initial volume (>30 mL)
• Irregular hematoma shape
• Blend sign on NCCT
IVH & Hydrocephalus Management
EVD Indications
ICH or IVH with hydrocephalus causing decreased consciousness: EVD recommended to reduce mortality.
Large IVH with impaired consciousness: EVD preferred over medical management alone.
IVH-Specific Management
GCS >3 with primary IVH or IVH extension from supratentorial ICH <30 mL requiring EVD: EVD + thrombolytic is reasonable to reduce mortality.
Functional outcome benefit from EVD + thrombolytic is uncertain.
Neuroendoscopic evacuation + EVD benefit is uncertain.
ICH Disposition & Goals of Care
Surgical Indications
Cerebellar ICH with mass effect
: urgent Neurosurgery evaluation for suboccipital decompression; obstructive hydrocephalus and/or brainstem compression commonly increase urgency and may require EVD.
Supratentorial ICH:
routine craniotomy for outcome benefit is uncertain.
Deteriorating supratentorial ICH:
craniotomy may be considered as a lifesaving measure.
Goals-of-Care Guidance
No preexisting limits:
aggressive care and postpone new DNAR/withdrawal until at least the second full facility day.
Do not limit other medical/surgical interventions solely due to DNAR unless explicitly specified.
Early palliative care for symptom management and shared decision making.
Seizure Management in ICH
Treat clinical seizures with antiseizure medication.
Treat electrographic seizures in impaired consciousness.
Continuous EEG (24 hours) is reasonable for unexplained abnormal or fluctuating mental status.
No prophylactic antiseizure medication in patients without seizures.
Anticoagulation Restart After ICH
When and how to restart anticoagulation in patients with AF who had an ICH. Individualize based on ICH location, etiology, and stroke risk.
Deep ICH (Favorable for Restart)
• Lower rebleed risk (~2%/yr) vs lobar
• Restart DOAC at
4-8 weeks
(AHA 2022)
• Consider earlier (2-4 wks) if high CHA₂DS₂-VASc (≥6) and stable imaging
• Prefer apixaban or edoxaban (lower ICH rates)
Lobar ICH (Higher Rebleed Risk)
• Often CAA-associated — rebleed ~7-10%/yr
• Delay restart to
≥8 weeks
or consider alternatives
•
LAA closure (Watchman)
: reasonable if CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥3 + high rebleed risk (STROKE-CLOSE)
• If restarting, use lowest effective DOAC dose
Key Evidence
•
SoSTART (2021):
OAC restart associated with lower recurrent stroke without excess ICH (observational)
•
APACHE-AF (2021):
Apixaban vs no anticoagulation — underpowered, trend favoring restart
•
PRESTIGE-AF (2024):
DOAC restart reduced ischemic events without significant increase in ICH
•
STROKE-CLOSE (2024):
LAA closure (Watchman) showed lower composite endpoint vs DOAC in ICH patients with AF (HR 0.61). Particularly beneficial for lobar ICH with high rebleed risk.
•
DOAC preferred over warfarin
for restart (lower ICH risk: 0.3-0.5%/yr vs 1%/yr)
Do NOT Restart If
• Multiple lobar ICH or severe CAA burden on MRI
• Ongoing hematoma expansion
• Uncontrolled hypertension
• Patient/family preference against restart
