Cerebral Venous Thrombosis (CVT) Management
AHA/ASA 2024 Guidelines — CSBP 2024
Acute Management Checklist
Anticoagulation initiated
LMWH or UFH, even with hemorrhagic infarction (Class I, LOE B-NR)
ICP assessment/management
HOB 30°, acetazolamide if needed, LP drainage for visual impairment
Seizure management addressed
Treat acute seizures with levetiracetam; routine prophylaxis NOT recommended (AHA/ASA). EEG if supratentorial lesion with altered consciousness.
Hematology consult / thrombophilia workup
Factor V Leiden, prothrombin mutation, protein C/S, APLA
Treatment Timeline & Escalation
Acute (Day 0-14)
• Therapeutic LMWH or UFH
• Even with hemorrhagic infarction (Class I)
• ICP management if needed
• Seizure treatment (not prophylaxis)
Subacute (Week 2-4)
• Transition to warfarin (INR 2-3) or DOAC
• DOAC reasonable if no APS (Class IIb, ACTION-CVT)
• Repeat imaging (MRV) at 3-6 months
Duration (3-12 mo)
• Provoked + resolved: 3-6 months
• Unprovoked: 6-12 months
• Recurrent VTE or severe thrombophilia: indefinite
• APS confirmed: warfarin (not DOAC)
Escalation Triggers
• Worsening despite anticoag → EVT consultation
• Severe ICP with visual loss → LP / shunt
• Herniation risk → decompressive craniectomy
• Refractory seizures → continuous EEG
Saposnik G et al. Stroke 2024. AHA/ASA CVT Scientific Statement. ACTION-CVT: Yaghi S et al. Stroke 2022;53:728-738. PMID: 35143325.
Risk Factor Assessment
Prothrombotic
• Factor V Leiden mutation
• Prothrombin G20210A
• Protein C/S deficiency
• Antithrombin III deficiency
• Antiphospholipid syndrome
• Hyperhomocysteinemia
Acquired
• OCP / hormonal therapy
• Pregnancy / puerperium
• Malignancy
• Infection (mastoiditis, sinusitis)
• IBD / nephrotic syndrome
• Dehydration / recent surgery
Diagnostic Imaging
First-line
•
MRI + MRV
(preferred)
• CT venography if MRI unavailable
• Non-contrast CT: cord sign, dense triangle sign, hemorrhagic venous infarct
Follow-up
• Repeat MRV at 3-6 months
• Assess for recanalization
• Guide duration of anticoagulation
Endovascular Therapy Consideration
Consider EVT if clinical deterioration despite adequate anticoagulation:
• Mechanical thrombectomy ± local thrombolysis
• Class IIb recommendation (LOE C-LD)
• Contact interventional neuroradiology early
• Decompressive craniectomy if impending herniation (Class IIa)

CVT in Special Populations
Special-Population Flags
Pregnancy / postpartum
Prefer LMWH/UFH. Avoid DOACs during pregnancy and breastfeeding.
APS confirmed
Use warfarin (INR 2-3) for long-term therapy; avoid DOACs.
Active cancer
Coordinate with hematology/oncology for agent interactions and procedures.
Severe thrombophilia
High recurrence profile; extended/indefinite anticoagulation often needed.
Standard CVT profile
Acute agent:
LMWH or UFH (weight-based).
Long-term plan:
Transition agent based on provoked vs unprovoked profile and bleeding risk.
Duration target:
3-6 months (provoked), 6-12 months (unprovoked), longer if recurrent risk.
Pregnancy / Postpartum
• LMWH throughout pregnancy (UFH also acceptable)
• Warfarin contraindicated in 1st trimester, acceptable postpartum
• DOACs contraindicated in pregnancy/breastfeeding
• Future pregnancy not contraindicated, but prophylactic LMWH recommended
Pediatric CVT
• Anticoagulation recommended (LMWH or UFH)
• Treat underlying infection aggressively
• 3-6 months anticoagulation typical
• Thrombophilia workup recommended
