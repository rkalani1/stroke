Vasospasm Monitoring & DCI Management
Monitoring Protocol
•
Daily TCD: Days 3-14 (peak vasospasm window)
•
MCA velocity >120 cm/s = mild spasm
•
MCA velocity >200 cm/s or Lindegaard ratio >6 = severe
•
Neuro checks q1h for first 14 days
•
CTP if new deficit or rising TCD velocities
DCI Treatment Escalation
1
Euvolemia + induced hypertension:
Phenylephrine or norepinephrine to raise MAP 20-25% above baseline
2
No improvement:
Endovascular rescue — intra-arterial verapamil/nicardipine
3
Refractory:
Balloon angioplasty for proximal vasospasm

Rebleeding Risk & Aneurysm Securing
Rebleeding Risk Timeline

~15% (peak risk)

~5-10%

~1-2%/day

~0.5%/day declining
Securing Strategy
Coiling (endovascular):
Preferred for posterior circulation, elderly, poor-grade, basilar tip. Class I.
Clipping (surgical):
Consider for MCA, large/wide-necked aneurysms, concurrent hematoma needing evacuation.
Timeline:
Secure within 24h of presentation (Class I, LOE B-NR).
Risk Factors for Rebleeding
• Unsecured aneurysm (most important)
• Poor clinical grade (H&H 4-5)
• Systolic BP >160 mmHg
• Large aneurysm size (>10 mm)
• Posterior circulation location
