## 2. P1 — Visual / "Claude Design" lift (make it striking without a rewrite)
The monolith makes a ground-up redesign risky; the **highest-leverage, lowest-risk** path is the
design-token layer + the 2-3 hero surfaces, which cascade everywhere.

1. **Typography** — keep the serif+mono concept but commit to a *distinctive* pairing. Suggested
   (clinical-but-characterful, avoid Inter/Roboto/Space Grotesk): display **Fraunces** or **Newsreader**;
   UI/body **Geist Sans** or **IBM Plex Sans**; data/eyebrows **IBM Plex Mono**. Load via `@font-face`
   with `font-display: swap`, self-hosted for offline-PWA correctness.
2. **Color & depth** — one dominant (the teal already in use) + one sharp time-critical accent. Add a
   consistent **acuity scale** (critical/caution/go) used identically across BP, glucose, time windows.
   Give the home hero atmosphere: a subtle gradient-mesh or 2% grain overlay + layered shadows instead of
   flat fills.
3. **Signature element** — the one memorable thing: an animated **door-to-needle / last-known-well ring
   or countdown** on the home overview. High clinical signal, visually distinctive, on-brand.
4. **Motion** — one orchestrated page-load stagger (`animation-delay`) on the overview tiles; tile hover
   states. Gate everything behind `prefers-reduced-motion` (non-negotiable for clinical users).
5. **Spatial** — the ≥1280px two-pane is good; tighten to a strict 4/8px spacing rhythm and an
   asymmetric editorial header. Keep density refined (attending audience), not toy-like.
