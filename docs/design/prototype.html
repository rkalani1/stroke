OCEANIC-STROKERCT · phase 3verified
Apr 2026added 05-30
Asundexian for secondary stroke prevention
N Engl J Med 20262° prevention · factor XIa
Design
n=12,327; noncardioembolic stroke / high-risk TIA with atherosclerosis ≤72 h; asundexian 50 mg + antiplatelet vs placebo + antiplatelet
Result
Ischemic stroke 6.2% vs 8.4% (HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.65–0.84; p<0.001) · major bleeding 1.9% vs 1.7% (NS)
What it changes
First positive phase-3 factor XIa inhibitor for 2° prevention — ~26% relative ischemic-stroke reduction on top of antiplatelets, without more major bleeding. Could reshape noncardioembolic prevention.

TRIDENTRCTverified
Apr 2026added 05-30
Three low-dose antihypertensives in a single pill after intracerebral hemorrhage
N Engl J Med 2026ICH · 2° prevention
Design
n=1,670; prior ICH, SBP 130–160; triple pill (telmisartan/amlodipine/indapamide, low-dose) vs placebo + standard care
Result
Recurrent stroke 4.6% vs 7.4% (HR 0.61, 95% CI 0.41–0.92; p=0.02) · SBP 127 vs 138 · major CV events 6.6% vs 9.8%
What it changes
A simplified low-dose triple pill after ICH lowers recurrent stroke and CV events — supports intensive, simplified BP-lowering (watch ~14% discontinuation, mainly creatinine rise).
Certainty · HighPubMed ↗
ATLASIPD meta-analysisverified
May 2026added 05-30
Thrombectomy for large-core stroke up to 24 h — central-imaging IPD meta-analysis
Lancet 2026Acute · large core
Design
6 trials, n=1,886; ASPECTS ≤5 or core ≥50 mL within 24 h; EVT vs medical management
Result
90-d mRS shift aGenOR 1.63 (95% CI 1.42–1.88; p<0.0001) · mortality 31.1% vs 37.3% (aRR 0.82) · sICH 1.1% vs 1.0%
What it informs
Consolidates EVT benefit across large-core strata to 24 h; benefit sustained except very extensive cores (≥150 mL) beyond 6 h, where evidence remains limited.
Certainty · HighPubMed ↗
TAPISRCTverified
May 2026added 05-30
Ticagrelor + aspirin DAPT with IV thrombolysis in ischaemic stroke
Lancet 2026Acute · reperfusion adjunct
Design
n=1,382; thrombolysis-treated AIS, NIHSS 4–10; aspirin + ticagrelor within 6 h vs placebo (DAPT days 2–7)
Result
mRS 0–1 at 90 d 68.7% vs 62.0% (RR 1.11, 95% CI 1.03–1.20; p=0.009) · sICH 0.9% vs 0.7%
What it informs
Early oral DAPT within 6 h as an adjunct to thrombolysis improved excellent outcomes in moderate stroke — a strategy long considered contraindicated; small sICH risk not excluded.
Certainty · ModeratePubMed ↗

Adjunctive intra-arterial alteplase after successful thrombectomy

DISTALRCT · 12-moverified
May 2026added 05-30
Thrombectomy for medium / distal vessel occlusion — 12-month outcomes
Lancet Neurol 2026Acute · MeVO
Design
n=543; M2/M3-4, A1-3, P1-3 occlusion, median NIHSS 6; EVT + BMT vs BMT alone
Result
12-mo mRS adj cOR 0.81 (95% CI 0.59–1.12; p=0.20) · survival HR 1.46 (NS) — no benefit
What it changes
No long-term benefit of EVT in mild–moderate medium/distal vessel occlusion — supports NOT routinely offering thrombectomy in this population.

DIRECT ANGIORCT · haltedverified
Apr 2026added 05-30
Direct-to-angio vs imaging-first transfer in severe stroke
Lancet Neurol 2026Workflow · triage
Design
n=115 (stopped early for safety); suspected LVO ≤85 y within 5 h; direct-to-angio vs imaging-first
Result
sICH 15% vs 0% (adj OR 11.0) · functional independence 36% vs 42% (NS) — harm, no benefit
What it informs
Bypassing pre-EVT imaging increased sICH without benefit and was halted early — cautions against direct-to-angio in suspected LVO (small n limits precision).

STABLEDRCTverified
Apr 2026added 05-30
Catheter ablation + anticoagulation for secondary stroke prevention in AF
JAMA Neurol 20262° prevention · AF
Design
n=249; nonvalvular AF + prior ischemic stroke on edoxaban; standard therapy + ablation vs standard therapy
Result
Composite 5.6% vs 4.9%/yr (HR 1.11, 95% CI 0.62–2.01) — no reduction; 2 ablation-related SAEs
What it informs
Adding ablation to anticoagulation did not reduce recurrent events in post-stroke AF (underpowered, low event rate) — does not support routine ablation for 2° prevention.
Certainty · ModeratePubMed ↗
